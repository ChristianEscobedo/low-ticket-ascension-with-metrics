# Millionaire Mindshift - Beginner VSL Script & Scene Plan

A second VSL written for a newer, less sophisticated audience. Same offer and same method as the main doc, but for everyday people who feel stuck with money and are new to this world. Lower awareness, simpler words, more heart.

Pair this with the page at `/millionaire-mindshift/vsl-script-starter`.

---

## 0. How to use this doc

Same shape as the main VSL doc. Every chapter gives you four things:

- **VO** - the words spoken. Read it slow.
- **SCENE** - the AI b-roll prompt for that beat.
- **TEXT** - the on-screen words.
- **WHY** - what the beat is doing.

### Voice rules (locked)

Same rules as the main doc. No dashes. No "not just X, but Y." Fifth grade words only. Short sentences. The first line names the promise and the person. For this audience, go even simpler and warmer. Less mechanism talk, more real life. Lead with "this is not your fault."

### Who this is for

Everyday people who work hard and still feel broke. Newer to personal growth. They have not tried ten mindset programs. They are not founders chasing the next zero. They just want money to stop slipping away. Talk to that person.

## 1. The 80% (for the beginner audience)

### Mechanism (kept simple)
**The Subconscious Reset.** Four plain steps: **Find, Test, Clear, Replace.** Find the hidden money rule in the back of your mind. Test it with a simple body check so you stop guessing. Clear it. Replace it with a rule that lets money stay. The surprise that makes people believe is the body check. Your own arm shows you the hidden rule, so it stops feeling like wishful thinking.

### New Cause (why nothing worked)
You were told to work harder, make a budget, and think positive. None of it stuck. The real problem is not your effort. It is a hidden money setting in the back of your mind, put there when you were small. You can not work your way out of a setting. You have to change the setting.

### Deeper Core (the wound)
You learned about money as a kid by watching. "We can't afford that." A worried face when a bill showed up. Quiet fights behind a door. Money felt scary, or short. You did not pick that. But your mind saved it as a rule, and that rule still runs today.

### Hidden Enemy
The money setting. Some call it a money thermostat. Set low, it quietly pulls you back to broke every time you get a little ahead. It is not you. It is the setting running you.

### Smoking Gun (simple proof)
A woman who was broke her whole life saved her first one thousand dollars in a single month. A mom paid off a card she had carried for years. A man too scared to ask for a raise finally asked, and got it. Same paycheck. One thing changed on the inside.

### Authority
Christian and Amber. A married couple who learned the hard way that more money did not fix the money stress. So they found a way to change the setting itself, and tried it on their own lives first.

### Symbols and dog whistles
The money thermostat. The hidden setting in the back of your mind. Money slipping through your fingers like sand. The rule you never chose. "Why is money always so hard for me" turning into "oh, that is why."

### 6 positioning points (use as ad openers)
1. You work hard, but money is always tight.
2. It is not your fault. You were taught that money is hard.
3. Your mind has a money setting from childhood.
4. You can not budget your way out of a money setting.
5. A simple body check shows you the hidden rule.
6. Change the setting and money stops slipping away.

### Archetypes in the room
- The Tired Worker: long hours, little to show, needs to hear "not your fault."
- The Worried Parent: wants to break the cycle for the kids.
- The Hopeful Dreamer: wants more, scared to want it out loud.
- The Burned Skeptic: tried budgets and books, needs the body-check proof.

### Neurochemistry target (85% dopamine, 15% cortisol)
Open with warm recognition (dopamine). One soft hit of "this is costing you" (cortisol). Then back to hope, the simple method, and proof (dopamine, serotonin). Never park in shame.

## 2. MAIN VSL (~3:00, beginner cut)

Job: take a tired, money-stressed person from "that is me" to "click the button" using one simple idea and real-life proof. No jargon. Warm, plain, hopeful.

---

### CH1 - Warm Hook (0:00 to 0:20)

**VO:** If you work hard but money is always tight, this is for you. You are not bad with money. You are not lazy. You are not broken. There is a hidden setting in the back of your mind that keeps pulling you back to broke. And it was put there when you were a kid, before you even knew what money was.

**SCENE:** A tired person sits at a kitchen table at night, a small stack of bills in front of them, phone in hand showing a low bank balance. Warm lamp light, soft and intimate. Slow push in on their face.

**TEXT:** It is not your fault.

**WHY:** Mirror Neuron Hook + Positioning (points 1 and 2). Removes shame in the first breath. Dopamine through recognition, one soft touch of cortisol on "broke."

---

### CH2 - Open Loop + Simple Proof (0:20 to 0:42)

**VO:** A woman who was broke her whole life saved her first one thousand dollars in a single month. She did not get a new job. She did not win anything. She changed one small thing on the inside. And once you see what it was, you will start to see it in your own life too.

**SCENE:** Close on a hand dropping folded cash into a glass jar that slowly fills up. Warm gold light, a hopeful little smile reflected in the glass. Simple and real.

**TEXT:** $1,000 saved. Same paycheck.

**WHY:** Smoking Gun + Open Loop, scaled to a believable, relatable number. Curiosity gap pulls them forward. Dopamine.

---

### CH3 - Deeper Core (0:42 to 1:08)

**VO:** Here is something most people never hear. When you were little, you learned how money works by watching. Maybe you heard, we can't afford that. Maybe you saw a worried face when a bill came in. Maybe money felt scary in your house, or there was never quite enough. You did not choose any of that. But your mind wrote it down as a rule. Money is hard. And that rule has been running quietly ever since.

**SCENE:** Dreamlike, warm-dim memory: a child sits on the stairs watching two parents at a kitchen table, worried over bills. Soft haze, nostalgic. Slow dissolve to that same child, now grown, sitting alone with their own bills.

**TEXT:** A rule you never chose.

**WHY:** Deeper Core + Hidden Enemy. Oxytocin through gentle truth. Names the wound so they feel seen, not blamed.

---

### CH4 - New Cause (1:08 to 1:36)

**VO:** So you try to fix it the normal way. Work more hours. Make a budget. Read a money book. Say nice things in the mirror. And it helps. For about a week. Then you are right back where you started. Here is why. The problem was never how hard you try. It is that hidden setting. You can not budget your way out of a setting. You have to change the setting itself.

**SCENE:** A table covered with a budget notebook, a calculator, and a money book under warm light. Then a slow close-up of coins and sand slipping through open fingers, no matter how tight the hand tries to hold.

**TEXT:** You can't budget your way out of a setting.

**WHY:** New Cause + permission to stop blaming yourself. Reframes past failure so it is not their fault. Dopamine through relief.

---

### CH5 - Mechanism, Simple (1:36 to 2:14)

**VO:** We are Christian and Amber. We used to think more money would finally fix the stress. It never did. So we found a way to reach that hidden setting and change it. We call it the Subconscious Reset. Four simple steps. Find the rule. Test it. Clear it. Put in a new one. The part that surprises everyone is the test. We use a simple body check. Your own arm shows you the hidden rule, so you are not guessing anymore. Then we clear it, and we set the dial higher, so money can finally stay.

**SCENE:** Christian and Amber on camera, warm and real (use real footage here). Cut to a simple wall thermostat dial turning from low to higher, glowing gold. Insert a close shot of a forearm doing a gentle muscle-test press, the arm holding strong, then dropping.

**TEXT:** Find. Test. Clear. Replace.

**WHY:** Mechanism + Authority. The body check is the disbelief-killer, made simple. The thermostat symbol is easy for a beginner to feel. Dopamine through a clear, doable path.

---

### CH6 - Real-Life Proof (2:14 to 2:36)

**VO:** A mom paid off a credit card she had carried for years. A guy who was too scared to ask for a raise finally asked, and got it the same week. A woman who always spent every last dollar started keeping money for the first time in her life. Same people. Same paycheck. The only thing that changed was the setting.

**SCENE:** A warm, quick montage: scissors cutting a credit card in half, a friendly handshake over a desk, coins and bills filling a jar a little higher. Soft gold text cards with first names float in and out. Real clips if you have them.

**TEXT:** Same paycheck. New setting.

**WHY:** Proof Stack + Confirmation Bias. Three small, believable wins build belief and serotonin. People like me can do this too.

---

### CH7 - Offer (2:36 to 2:56)

**VO:** We put the whole thing into one simple program. How to find your money rule. The body check. The clearing. The new rule that lets money stay. Plus short audios you can play in the car or before bed to keep it going while you live your life. The whole thing is twenty seven dollars. Less than one dinner out.

**SCENE:** A simple, friendly program mockup glows into frame on a warm wooden table: a phone showing the lessons, headphones beside it. A price stamp lands: $27, with a struck-through $197 above it.

**TEXT:** The whole method. $27.

**WHY:** Offer Anchor + Stack. Price compared to a dinner, easy for a beginner to feel. The offer is the natural next step, not a hard pivot.

---

### CH8 - Two Roads (2:56 to 3:10)

**VO:** So here are your two roads. Keep working hard and watching money slip away, year after year, the same way it always has. Or take one hour, change the setting, and let money finally start to stay.

**SCENE:** A simple fork in a warm-lit path. The left side loops back like a treadmill going nowhere. The right side opens toward a bright, calm horizon. Camera drifts slowly toward the right.

**TEXT:** Two roads. One choice.

**WHY:** Tale of Two Futures + Now or Never. Real consequence, not fake scarcity. One soft hit of cortisol on the loop, then dopamine on the open road.

---

### CH9 - CTI Close (3:10 to 3:24)

**VO:** If a part of you just went, that is me, listen to that part. It has been waiting a long time for someone to say this is not your fault. Click the button. Start the reset. Let's change the setting, and let money stay.

**SCENE:** Back to the person from CH1 at the kitchen table, but calmer now, a full money jar beside them, a small steady smile. They reach toward the camera (toward the button). Warm gold light blooms.

**TEXT:** That's me. Start the reset.

**WHY:** Call To Identity, not call to action. Hands the choice to the part of them that already raised its hand. Warm, hopeful close.

---

### Optional ad pre-roll (5 second cold open)

**VO:** Yeah, this is an ad. Watch it anyway, because it explains the real reason money keeps feeling so hard.

**WHY:** 4th Wall Break + Pattern Interrupt for paid traffic. Names the ad to buy attention, then lands on the core promise.

## 3. Seedance b-roll prompts (beginner cut)

Built for ByteDance Seedance (1.0 pro). One camera move and one clear action per clip. Generate at 1080p, 5s per clip, then trim. Render 16:9 for the VSL and 9:16 for ads. This cut is warmer and more "home and kitchen" than the main doc, to fit an everyday audience.

**STYLE SUFFIX (paste at the end of every prompt):**
> cinematic, shallow depth of field, 24fps, subtle film grain, slow deliberate camera move, warm and homey, soft golden light, deep shadows, fine dust in the air, high contrast, photorealistic, no text or logos in frame.

**CHARACTER LOCK (reuse for the everyday person):**
> the same ordinary hardworking person in their 30s to 40s, simple casual clothes (plain tee or hoodie), kind tired face. Keep the same face, wardrobe, and warm grade in every shot.

1. **Kitchen table (hero):** An ordinary person sits at a kitchen table at night, a small stack of bills in front of them, phone in hand showing a low bank balance, warm lamp light. Their face is tired and worried. Slow push in on the face.

2. **The jar fills:** Close on a hand dropping folded cash into a clear glass jar that slowly fills, a hopeful little smile reflected in the glass. Warm light. Slow tilt up the jar.

3. **Child on the stairs:** Dreamlike, warm-dim memory of a child sitting on a staircase, watching two worried parents at a kitchen table covered in bills. Soft haze, nostalgic. Gentle slow drift forward.

4. **Slipping away:** A table with a budget notebook, calculator, and a money book under warm light, then a slow close-up of coins and fine sand slipping through tightly closed fingers. Macro, slight push in.

5. **Founders (AI alt):** A warm, ordinary couple in their 30s seated close together facing the camera in a cozy, softly lit room, calm and kind. (Use real Christian and Amber footage if available; this is the fallback.) Slow gentle push in.

6. **The thermostat:** A simple round wall thermostat dial turns slowly from a low number to a higher one, glowing warm gold as it rises. Macro, static with a slight zoom.

7. **Body check:** Close shot of a forearm and hand in a gentle muscle-test press, the arm holding strong, then dropping. Simple and physical, warm light. Static macro with a slight push in.

8. **Small wins:** A warm quick montage: scissors cutting a credit card in half, a friendly handshake over a desk, coins and bills filling a jar a little higher. Soft focus, handheld-gentle.

9. **Offer reveal:** A friendly program mockup glows into frame on a warm wooden table: a phone showing simple lessons, headphones beside it, a price stamp landing of a glowing number over a smaller struck-through one. Slow orbit.

10. **Two roads:** A simple fork in a warm-lit path at dusk; the left path loops back on itself like a treadmill, the right path opens toward a calm bright horizon. Camera drifts slowly toward the right.

11. **Calm close:** The same person from shot 1 at the kitchen table, calmer now, a full money jar beside them, a small steady smile, reaching gently toward the camera. Warm gold light bloom. Slow push in.
